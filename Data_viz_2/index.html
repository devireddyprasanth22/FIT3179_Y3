Tourism over the years

Tourism has witnessed a remarkable surge in recent decades, driven in part by the increased accessibility of international travel Before the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, the number of international visits had more than doubled since the turn of the 21st century. This upswing in tourism reflects the growing global interest in exploring new cultures and destinations. Whether it's the allure of iconic landmarks, the discovery of local traditions, or the pursuit of unique experiences, tourism has become a dynamic force connecting people across borders.

Tourism and Travel metrics

There are several reasons why people might want to visit a country but they can be categorised under either Personal or Business. The visualisation on the right is able to offer essential insights in the motivations behind international travel, providing valusable information to travellers and relevant stakeholders. It can be noted that for most countries, the purpose of visit tends to be for personal reasons

The number of tourism employees per sector within the tourism industry, including Accommodation, Food and Beverage (F&B), Passenger Transport, Travel Agencies, and other related segments, is a vital metric reflecting the workforce distribution within this dynamic sector. These statistics reveal the complex web of employment opportunities created by the tourism industry, spanning from hotels and restaurants to transportation services and travel agencies. It highlights the interconnected nature of the tourism ecosystem, where the success and sustainability of one sector often depend on the support and collaboration of others.

Tourism GDP as a proportion of GDP

he visualization of Tourism direct GDP as a proportion of total GDP serves as a crucial metric for understanding the economic significance of the tourism industry within a given country. It offers a clear insight into the role that tourism plays in the overall economic landscape, indicating the extent to which a nation's GDP is dependent on the tourism sector.

A high proportion suggests that tourism is a major driver of the economy, potentially leading to economic vulnerabilities during tourism downturns or disruptions. Conversely, a lower proportion may indicate a more diversified economy less reliant on tourism.
